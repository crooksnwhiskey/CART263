The project I chose to review was: https://aydanmcgrail.github.io/cart263_FinalProject/
clicking on the link to this game was insane for the first time. I was not expecting such a professional looking game from this assignment. What really impressed me was the fully functional ai opponent. It's such a complcated looking thing to accomplish but looking at the code it actually makes so much sense. Using the lookat function to always have the opponent look at you adds so much, especially when paired with the constant movement towards the player. Perhaps the most impressive thing was the way that the opponents model was able to move their arms separately from the whole body. Looking at the code I still cant figure that one out. I can see that they used a sphere to fake a hand and the radius acts as the collision detection for the damage, however I cant figure out what allows the arm to move and bend freely from the body. 
The general atmosphere of the game is really well done aswell. although its simple, the boxing arena felt like it was well though out, with the lights being nice and the giant head above being very daunting. I'm not entirely sure if this is what they were going for, but their game made me feel like I was the victim of bullying. Every thing in that game, including the design choices are slightly unnerving and it makes me feel uneasy.
other small things contributed to the realism of the game aswell, such as the opponent having dilogue, aswell as the hit sounds and bloody visuals. all these small details add up to enhance the experience. I find the music very fitting aswell, however I think the loop cuts off a little too ealy. I also like the ads that are huge and in your face. I get that it's supposed to be a satirical representation of how the advertisment system we live in is getting ridiculous, but if you look at a UFC match, it's actually not far off at all.
The last thing That I find super advanced is the opponents health bar. By using planes they were able to create a 3d health bar which is much more captivating than having a static one overlayed on top of the screen. They used the same lookat function so that the health bar is always visible to the player, which gives the game a very polished feeling.
Overall, the game has many advanced areas and is definitely a project to be proud of. Although it already feels quite complete, I feel like this is a project that is a very good template to come back to later and do some little upgrades. If they had the time, they could add things like new opponents, a ladder system to climb, and maybe even some more punching mechanics.
